Measuring the Distance to Your Star

You're now ready to use the angle and baseline measurements you made previously to compute the distance to your star. Since you took photos from the left and right you'll get two different distances.

The distance from your 'Left measurements'

With the photos you took from the left and middle positions, you were able to measure the left baseline and parallax angles. Enter those values here:


A bit of trigonometry tells us that the distance to the 'star' is given by:
d = bL / tan pL

The distance from your 'Right measurements'

With the photos you took from the right and middle positions, you were able to measure the right baseline and parallax angles. Enter those values here:


A bit of trigonometry tells us that the distance to the 'star' is given by:
d = bR / tan pR

You've finished computing the distance to your star! Write down your values. You'll upload them on the next (and final) page.
